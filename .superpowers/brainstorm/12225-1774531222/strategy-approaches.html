TAJIRI Growth Engine — 3 Strategic Approaches

Each approach packages the addiction loops, gossip virality, and creator payments differently. Click your preference.

A

The Flywheel (Recommended)

One unified system where all three strategies reinforce each other.

Creators post → AI packages trending content into gossip threads → Viewers consume gossip → Engagement earns creators money → Creators post more.

Addiction loops are tied to the gossip cycle (daily digest, trending alerts, "this thread is blowing up"). Creator payments are weighted by virality (gossip-triggering content earns more). Gossip engine IS the feed algorithm.

Pros

  • Self-reinforcing — each part makes the others stronger
  • Single system to build and maintain
  • Creates unique "TAJIRI culture" (gossip + money)
  • Network effects compound fastest

Cons

  • More complex initial design
  • If gossip engine underperforms, everything suffers
  • Harder to A/B test individual components
B

The Layer Cake

Three independent systems stacked on top of each other.

Build addiction loops first (streaks, rewards, notifications). Then add gossip engine as a separate discovery tab. Then add creator payments as a wallet feature.

Addiction loops are generic gamification (XP, levels, streaks). Gossip engine is its own "Trending" section. Creator payments are calculated independently from virality.

Pros

  • Simplest to build — each piece is independent
  • Can ship each layer separately
  • Easy to A/B test and measure each layer
  • If one fails, others still work

Cons

  • No synergy — feels like 3 bolted-on features
  • Gamification without content purpose feels hollow
  • Slower network effects
C

The Dual Engine

Two connected systems: Content Engine (gossip + addiction) and Money Engine (payments + marketplace).

Content Engine handles feed algorithm, gossip curation, engagement loops, and notifications. Money Engine handles creator earnings, shop, tips, and subscriptions. They connect through "engagement score" — a universal currency.

Addiction loops feed into engagement score. Gossip amplifies engagement score. Payments convert engagement score to money.

Pros

  • Clean separation of concerns
  • Content team and monetization team can work independently
  • Engagement score is a clear, measurable metric
  • Moderate synergy between systems

Cons

  • "Engagement score" abstraction may feel artificial
  • Two systems to maintain
  • Less organic than the Flywheel

My Recommendation: A (The Flywheel)

TAJIRI's unique advantage is combining African social culture with creator monetization. The Flywheel makes gossip the product — not a feature. Creators are incentivized to make share-worthy, talk-about-it content because that's what earns the most. Viewers are hooked because the gossip is genuinely compelling and constantly refreshing. The money flows to where the attention goes.

This is how TikTok, Twitter, and YouTube all work at their core — one flywheel, not stacked features.
